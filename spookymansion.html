The Problem

This university project focused on designing a fictional vinyl cover for a lesser-known band we personally connected with.

I chose Spooky Mansion, a Los Angeles-based band originally from the Bay Area, known for blending surf rock, soul, and garage rock into a breezy, high-energy sound.

The challenge was to visually translate the duality within their music: relaxed and nostalgic on the surface, yet emotionally raw and psychedelic underneath.

The cover needed to avoid generic retro aesthetics while still capturing the warmth, movement, and spontaneity that define the band's sound and live performances.

The Idea

The concept started from the emotional contrast inside Spooky Mansion’s music.

Their sound feels bright, loose, and summery, while still carrying a subtle sense of melancholy and distortion.

I wanted the visual identity of the vinyl to reflect this balance between carefree energy and emotional haze.

The design draws inspiration from vintage surf culture, psychedelic posters, and 70s-inspired graphic experimentation.

Warm oranges and saturated yellows recreate the feeling of sunlight and heat, while turquoise tones introduce freshness and movement, echoing the ocean-inspired atmosphere of surf rock.

The Solution

The final vinyl cover combines bold color blocking, stylized illustration, and experimental typography into a cohesive visual system inspired by the sonic identity of the band.

The front cover focuses on immediacy and personality, using fluid forms and vibrant contrasts to communicate the energetic “surf-soul-rock-n-roll” vibe of Spooky Mansion.

The back cover simplifies the composition while maintaining the same palette and visual rhythm, creating a cleaner and more atmospheric layout.

The final result is a cover design that feels nostalgic, dynamic, and contemporary at the same time — visually translating the warmth, rhythm, and emotional spontaneity of the band’s music.
