Unseen Cat Studio

Brand Identity / 2026

100 Matteotti

Social Design / 2025

The Problem

The original identity for Unseen Cat Studio struggled with visibility and clarity. While the dark, atmospheric logo aligned conceptually with the studio’s focus on psychological horror, it relied too heavily on low contrast and subtle details. The cat figure was difficult to distinguish, especially at smaller sizes or in digital contexts, and the typography blended into the background. As a result, the logo communicated mood but lacked immediacy, scalability, and strong brand recognition.

The Idea

The redesign builds on the studio’s core theme of duality—comfort versus unease, seen versus unseen—using the cat as a symbolic anchor. The concept introduces a clearer visual metaphor: two overlapping cat silhouettes that suggest presence and absence at once. This dual imagery reflects the studio’s narrative approach, where familiar elements become unsettling. The idea was to preserve the eerie tone while making the identity more legible, iconic, and adaptable across platforms.

The Solution

The final logo adopts a high-contrast black-and-white palette to enhance readability and impact. The cat silhouette is simplified into a bold, recognizable shape, with sharp eyes acting as a focal point. The secondary, faded silhouette reinforces the theme of duality without adding visual noise. Typography is clean and modern, with a clear hierarchy that separates “Unseen Cat” from “Studio,” improving legibility at all scales. Overall, the redesign balances atmosphere and functionality, resulting in a more versatile and memorable brand identity.

The Problem

The memory of Giacomo Matteotti—and the historical weight of events like his opposition to fascism and his assassination in 1924—risks feeling distant for younger generations. Traditional commemorations often rely on formal language, archival imagery, and institutional tones that may not resonate with a youth audience. Organizations such as CGIL, ANPI, and local mutual aid societies like SOMS Settimo Torinese face the challenge of keeping historical memory alive in a way that feels relevant, immediate, and engaging—especially in public urban spaces.

The Idea

The project reimagines key moments in Matteotti’s life through a bold pop-art visual language. By using vibrant colors, simplified shapes, and graphic contrasts, the posters translate complex historical narratives into striking, contemporary images. This stylistic choice bridges past and present, borrowing from visual codes familiar to younger audiences (street art, digital illustration, poster culture). Instead of presenting Matteotti as a distant historical figure, the imagery frames him as a vivid, almost iconic presence—surrounded by scenes of political tension, personal life, and public mobilization.

The Solution

The result is a series of large-format posters and banners designed for public display— specifically installed in front of the CGIL headquarters in Settimo Torinese. This placement transforms a commemorative initiative into a visible, everyday encounter. Passersby, including younger viewers, are drawn in by the aesthetic first, then invited to reflect on the story behind it. The collaboration between CGIL, ANPI, and SOMS ensures both historical accuracy and civic relevance, while the pop-art approach functions as a gateway to engagement. By combining cultural memory with contemporary visual language, the project succeeds in turning commemoration into communication—making Matteotti’s legacy accessible, recognizable, and meaningful.
