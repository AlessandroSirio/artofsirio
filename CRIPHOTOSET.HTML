Photography — Photoset

Photoset
Italian Red Cross of Settimo Torinese

Year

2024

Client

Italian Red Cross of Settimo Torinese

Role

Art Direction, Photography, Editor

Duration

2 weeks

A series of photo sets documenting the events and activities of Croce Rossa Italiana — Comitato di Settimo Torinese. Each shoot captures the organisation's presence on the ground — combining reportage and institutional photography to build a visual archive that connects the human side of their work with the community they serve.

Concept

Creative Process

The Comitato di Settimo Torinese has been active in the local community since 1974 — and that presence is most visible in the moments it shows up. The photo sets document the organisation's events not as institutional records, but as human stories: volunteers, people, and community life caught in the field. The approach favours an observational, documentary eye over staged imagery — building a visual archive that reflects fifty years of genuine rootedness in the territory.

Brief

Produce a series of photo sets documenting the events and public activities of Croce Rossa Italiana — Comitato di Settimo Torinese, capturing the human side of the organisation's work and building a coherent visual presence across their communication channels.

Tools

Nikon D3310 Photoshop Lightroom

Deliverable

A series of edited photo sets, delivered in formats optimised for both digital publishing and archival use.

Process

Behind the mark

01

Research

Alignment with the client on the events to document, definition of the visual approach, and planning of the shooting schedule across the different occasions.

02

Shooting

On-site photography during the events, capturing both the organisation's public activities and the human moments behind them — with an observational, documentary approach.

03

Editing

Curation of the best shots from each session and post-production in Adobe Lightroom, ensuring visual consistency across the full series.

04

Delivery

Final edited photo sets delivered in formats optimised for digital publishing and archival use.
